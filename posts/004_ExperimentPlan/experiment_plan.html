Designing experiments in the lab takes a lot of effort and critical thinking. Additionally, you have to think of the feasibility and cost of such tasks. In my opinion, I find it difficult to be incredibly thorough why my experimental controls. For those that may not be as familiar with science experiments, controls are conditions that are kept constant. In essence, they act as benchmarks to compare the results of the experiment to. They help determine whether or not a change was significant or not. They help scientists determine if the changes in results are truly due to the factor being tested and not an external element.

My Thoughts

In terms of controls, Chat-GPT was able to identify most of what was needed. It was really interesting to see that the LLM recommend the same mouse breed that my lab actually does use in our research, mainly because there are tons of research mouse breeds used and it was able to guess the correct one. One thing that I wasn‚Äôt familiar with was the Transwell/Blood-Brain Barrier Assay. That was something we hadn‚Äôt even thought of because we have an established animal facility and can easily have access to animal studies. However, if we didn‚Äôt, this would be a good model to see the initial feasibility of neuroinvasion. Everything else seems very similar to the actual plan of my project!

Overall, given the correct background and initial questions, I think LLMs are capable to delivering a preliminary proposal. This is incredibly beneficial because it is difficult to create a complete and thorough experiment from the bottom up. This provides a solid foundation to build off of. I did want to add that there are some gaps in the process here, and something that the LLM doesn‚Äôt take into account is the capabilities and availabilities of the lab itself. Not every lab has access to flow cytometry, for example.
